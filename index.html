Screen Size

In most cases, size is the biggest factor when selecting a TV. To select a screen
size, you might want to consider the number of people that the TV at
a given time, where you are going to the place the TV, and pick the largest screen
size that will fill the avialable space without going over budget. The most popular
TV size today, considering price, space and the space avialable in the
typical living room, is between 50 and 65 inches.

calculating the best size of TV for your room

Consider the room in which the TV will sit. Measure the distance (in inches) between where you plan to mount the TV and where you plan to sit. To calculate the best size TV for your room, measure your viewing distance (in feet) and multiply that by 7.7. So if you have a 42-inch TV, you can sit 84-inches (7 feet) away for a comfortable and immersive experience. If you are looking for a 4k ultra-high definition television, the formula changes a bit. Because of the increased resolution, the recommended viewing distance is between 1 and 1.5 times the screen size. So if you buy a 50-inch TV, it would need to be placed between 50 to 75 inches away from where you'd sit.

LCD or LED or OLED or QLED?

Picture quality has remained an important part of the TV equation. OLED, QLED, LED, LCD are competing technologies that each offer a trade-off between price and picture quality. However, their basic capabilities are identical.

QLED: The Q in QLED stands for quantum, as in quantum dot LED TVs. This technologies use quantum dots (QD), semiconductor nanocrystals which can which can produce pure mono chromatic red, green and blue light. QLED TVs have a rich color volume and high light output and a better color for HDR sources. Most importantly, low power consumption makes QLED more than twice as power efficient as OLED at the same color point.

OLED: An organic light emitting diode (LED) in which the emissive electrolumuniscent layer is a film of organic compound that emits light inresponse to an electric current. OLEDs have large fields of view and do not require backlighting like LCDs. They can be so small that they can be used as individual pixels, millions of which occupy your TV screens, lightning up and shutting off totally independently. Becaus eof this flexibility. When an OLED is shut off it is completely off, which leads to a deeper and more natural shade of black.

LED: A light-emitting diode (LED) is a two-lead semiconductor light source. It is a p–n junction diode that emits light when activated. When a suitable current is applied to the leads, electrons are able to recombine with electron holes within the device, releasing energy in the form of photons. LED TVs produces more vivid images compared to fluorescent lights. LED TVs have the ability to create dark areas in the screen and control it, which is something that is missing in the traditional LCD TVs. Both edge lightning and full-array backlightning have the capacity to adjust black levels, allowing for darker blacks and significantly better dark scenes. Moreover, accuracy in backlighning for colored pictures is also better with LED TVs.


LCD: A liquid-crystal display (LCD) is a flat-panel display or other electronically modulated optical device that uses the light-modulating properties of liquid crystals. Image is perfectly shaped at the native resolution of the panel. The high peak intensity produces very bright images but has limited viewing angels.


Smart TVs: A smart TV is a television set with integrated and interactive web features. They have built in WiFi for connecting to internet-based services like Netflix for streaming videos, downloading on-demand movies, playing games or even posting to Facebook. The recent models can even search for content across streaming services and live programming on cable and on satelitte.


Curved Screen vs Flat Screen TVs

Curved TVs are sleek are offer a higher level of immersion to the viewers sitting in front of them by slightly expanding the perceptiblequantity of screen space for their diagonal size, while flat screen TVs are far more amendable to be hanged along any vertical space.

Inputs

HDMI Port: HDMI (High definition multimedia interface) serves as an efficient method to transfer video and audio data to HDMI ready TVs, from streaming media players, Blu-ray players, games consoles and more. HDMI carries both the picture and sound over one convinient cable, unlike traditional A/V ports which requires at least two seperate cables.

USB Port: TVs with USB ports allows you to plug in a hard drive, flash drive or other USB storage devices to view media files on your television set. These include photos, music and of course video files.
